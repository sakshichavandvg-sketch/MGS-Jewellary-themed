Our Founding Story

Where It All Began

In 1999, on the bustling P.J. Extension of Davanagere, a Senior Goldsmith opened the doors of MGS Jewellery with a single promise: to give every family in Davanagere access to pure, hallmarked gold at maker-direct prices — with zero compromise on quality.

Unlike large retail chains that source jewellery and add layers of markup, MGS was born as a goldsmith's workshop first. Every piece was crafted on-site, by hand, under the direct supervision of a master craftsman who understood that gold was not just commerce — it was a family heirloom, a sacred offering, a daughter's wedding dowry.

Over the years, that founding workshop grew into Davanagere's most trusted jewellery destination. But the philosophy never changed: transparent pricing, certified purity, and maker-direct value remain the cornerstones of everything we do.

Our Craft

The Art of the Goldsmith

Each piece that leaves MGS carries the mark of century-old techniques married with modern precision.

Wax-Casting & Lost-Wax Method

Traditional South Indian jewellery uses the lost-wax (cire perdue) casting method — where a wax model is encased in clay, the wax melted out, and molten gold poured in. Our master goldsmith has refined this technique over two decades, achieving intricate detail that machines cannot replicate.

Traditional Technique

Hand Filigree & Engraving

Filigree is the art of twisting fine gold wire into lace-like patterns. Our craftsmen hand-engrave temple motifs, floral designs, and peacock patterns directly onto gold — creating one-of-a-kind pieces that cannot be mass-produced. Every groove tells the story of the hand that made it.

Artisan Craft

Stone Setting & Jadau Work

Our goldsmiths are trained in Jadau setting — the royal Rajputana art of embedding uncut gemstones (kundan) into gold. We also set modern-cut rubies, emeralds, and certified diamonds with precision — ensuring stone and gold are in harmony for lifetimes.

Gemstone Excellence

Karatmeter Purity Testing

Every piece undergoes purity verification with calibrated XRF Karatmeter testing before being sent for BIS/HUID hallmarking. We never presume purity — we prove it. The hallmark stamp on your jewellery is backed by government certification and our own in-house quality assurance.

Quality Assurance

Meenakari Enamel Work

Meenakari is the ancient art of filling gold with vibrant enamel colours — peacocks, flowers, and geometric patterns brought to life. Our craftsmen source mineral-based enamel powders and fire them at precise temperatures, creating jewellery that is as much artwork as it is gold.
